Overview of the sport

Dragonboat is a water-racing sport. Dragonboat is a racing sport which makes it automatically competitive and fun to both watch and participate in. What makes it better is that it isn't just one person, it's 20 people (10 in small boat). In a normal race, the single best runner wins, but in dragonboat, the best 20 win. It's a 20 vs 20 sport, which is much more exciting than a 1 vs 1 one. Some people think dragonboat is a random sport, like that of water polo, handball, etc. However, not only is it the ultimate team sport (20 v 20), it's the ultimate water racing sport (20 v 20 on water) because you can go much faster. It's great for recreational and competitive levels because it's both a community sport and a sport that's pure practice and based on technical and small details. Dragonboat is popular in many countries, such as Canada, USA, Germany, China, Thailand, Hungary, and more.

This website is here as a way for teams to be able to convert 10 person boat (small boats) times to 20 person boat (standard boats) times, and vice versa. We will be trying to develop a conversion for open, mixed, and women's boats for 500m races. We will be comparing Premier (age category with no age restrictions) boat times because they will be more consistent. It's easier to have 10 high-skilled premiers and 20 high-skilled premiers than 10 high-skilled seniors and 20 high-skilled seniors since finding many high quality senior paddlers is hard to achieve, so the data will be more reflecting of the truth using premiers. This conversion is used to calculate your 500m times if your crew of 10 doubled or crew of 20 halved. This does not mean selecting the 10 best paddlers out of 20 but it does work for 10 paddlers converting to 20.

Analyzing the information, you can find out a lot about women, open, and mixed dragonboats. Importantly, time for the conversions. These are premier teams, so their 20 person speed to 10 person speed ratio will be much greater than your's, as adding 10 extra paddlers makes them go significantly faster. If your team is not as good (most teams), the difference won't be as great. Another important thing is that I will be using the numbers above because they are the most reliable numbers out there, but I have added an adjustment factor.

The Conversions

Mixed Crews

Small boat --> Standard boat: (Small boat time (seconds) / 1.163) + 3 to 5 seconds

Standard boat --> Small boat: (Standard boat time (seconds) X 1.163) - 3 to 5 seconds

Women Crews

Small boat --> Standard boat: (Small boat time (seconds) / 1.106) + 3 to 5 seconds

Standard boat --> Small boat: (Standard boat time (seconds) X 1.106) - 0 to 3 seconds

Open Crews

Small boat --> Standard boat: (Small boat time (seconds) / 1.133) + 3 to 5 seconds

Standard boat --> Small boat: (Standard boat time (seconds) X 1.133) - 3 to 5 seconds

IMPORTANT: THE BETTER YOUR TEAM, the more you add or subtract.

These conversions have been tested many times across many years and competitions, and the majority of the time, it works.
